François Englert, Nobel laureate and co-theorist of the Brout–Englert–Higgs mechanism, has passed away

A tribute to one of the theorists behind the mechanism explaining how fundamental particles acquire mass.

Source date
CERN article, 19 June 2026
Scientific context
François Englert and Robert Brout showed that fundamental particles could acquire mass through interaction with a field present throughout the Universe. Peter Higgs independently proposed the same mechanism, and the associated particle was discovered in 2012 by ATLAS and CMS at the LHC.
Why it matters
The Brout–Englert–Higgs mechanism is one of the central ideas of the Standard Model, connecting quantum field theory, symmetry breaking, and the origin of particle masses.
