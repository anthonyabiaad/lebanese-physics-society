Quantum for finance

From a financial decision to a quantum optimization problem

The founder, Anthony ABI AAD, worked with the French Qubits hackathon team on a project around portfolio optimization. The central question was how to select a set of assets while balancing expected return, risk, asset correlations, and practical constraints.

The quantum approach helped define the problem more clearly by translating a financial decision into a structured combinatorial optimization problem. Each asset can be represented by a binary variable: selected or not selected. Once written in this form, the problem can be mapped to a QUBO formulation and then to an Ising Hamiltonian, which is the natural language of many quantum optimization algorithms.

In that Hamiltonian, economic terms become physical terms: expected return contributes to the reward, covariance contributes to risk, portfolio-size constraints become penalty terms, and additional industrial constraints such as liquidity, transaction costs, and market impact can also be encoded. This makes the problem compatible with hybrid methods such as QAOA, where a quantum circuit explores candidate portfolios and a classical optimizer updates the circuit parameters.

What the project illustrates

  • How a finance problem can be written as a QUBO / Ising Hamiltonian.
  • How QAOA can be used to search over possible portfolio selections.
  • How quantum kernels can help with asset pre-selection and similarity analysis.
  • How AI modules and warm-start strategies can improve the optimization pipeline.
  • How quantum and classical methods can be combined rather than treated as competitors.

This is a research and learning prototype, not a production trading strategy or financial recommendation.

Optimization

Portfolio selection, risk-return trade-offs, allocation constraints, transaction costs, and market-impact terms can naturally lead to hard discrete optimization problems.

Quantum & AI pipeline

Quantum kernels, classical preprocessing, machine learning, warm-start states, and QAOA can be combined into a hybrid workflow for studying financial datasets.
